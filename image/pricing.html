How to do CPR?

The quick and easy method!

CPR is a life-saving technique used when someone's heart or breathing stops. First, ensure the area is safe and check if the person is unresponsive. Call emergency services immediately. Place the person on their back and start chest compressions by pressing hard and fast in the center of the chest, about 2 inches deep, at a rate of 100-120 compressions per minute. After 30 compressions, give 2 rescue breaths if you're trained. If not, continue chest compressions until help arrives. CPR helps maintain blood flow to vital organs until medical professionals can take over.

Snake Bit

If you’re bitten by a snake, stay calm and seek immediate medical help. Keep the affected limb immobilized and at or below heart level to slow venom spread. Avoid using a tourniquet or cutting the bite area. Do not try to suck out the venom. If possible, take note of the snake’s color and shape to help with identification. Get to the hospital as quickly as possible, as timely treatment is crucial.

What to Do if You Have Trouble Breathing:

If you're having trouble breathing, try to stay calm and sit in an upright position. Take slow, deep breaths, and try to relax. If you have asthma or another respiratory condition, use your inhaler or prescribed medication. If you continue to have difficulty breathing or experience chest pain, dizziness, or swelling, seek emergency medical help immediately. If possible, call for assistance and avoid physical exertion until help arrives.

Work together!

In life-threatening situations, every second counts, and working together can make all the difference. Whether it's performing CPR, providing first aid, or helping someone in distress, your actions can save lives. When we unite, we bring our strengths, knowledge, and support to those in need. Remember, you’re never alone in an emergency — together, we can act quickly and efficiently, ensuring the best possible outcome. So, let's stand strong and support each other, because in moments of crisis, teamwork truly saves lives.
